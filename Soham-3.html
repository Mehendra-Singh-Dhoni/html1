Interactive Internship Report by Soham Rokade (20240802534)

D Y Patil International University, Akurdi, Pune | Zilla Parishad Primary School, Shirgaon

The Problem

Rural primary schools often face a significant technology gap due to limited resource access and a lack of structured digital training. This hinders modern teaching methods and restricts students' preparedness for a competitive future.

Our Objectives

  • Introduce fundamental computer skills to primary students.
  • Train teachers in using digital tools for lesson planning.
  • Create engaging, interactive learning materials.
  • Assess and understand challenges in rural education.

Key Challenges Faced

  • Limited Infrastructure: Few computers restricted hands-on practice.
  • Time Constraints: Internship duration limited coverage of advanced topics.
  • Low Initial Exposure: Extra time needed for students new to computers.
  • Internet Availability: Unstable connectivity was a frequent hurdle.

Conclusion & Learnings

This internship proved that targeted digital literacy programs can have a profound impact. It reinforced the importance of creating simple, accessible learning materials and adapting to resource constraints. The experience highlighted that even short-term exposure to technology can significantly empower both students and teachers, paving the way for a more equitable learning landscape.
